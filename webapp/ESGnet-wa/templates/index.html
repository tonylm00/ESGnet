What is a
Service Value Network?

A Service Value Network (SVN) is a dynamic, interconnected system of organizations, individuals, and resources that collaborate to create, deliver, and enhance the value of services. In a company's SVN, various stakeholders such as partners, suppliers, customers, and competitors interact through multiple relationships like partnerships, investments, and customer connections. This network approach helps companies optimize their operations, innovate services, and respond effectively to market changes by leveraging the collective capabilities and expertise within the network. The goal is to maximize the overall value delivered to all participants, ultimately driving sustainable business growth and customer satisfaction.

What is ESG score?

An ESG score is a measure of a company's performance in three key areas: Environmental, Social, and Governance. These scores help investors, stakeholders, and consumers evaluate how well a company manages risks and opportunities related to sustainability and ethical practices. ESG scores are increasingly important as they reflect a company's commitment to responsible business practices, which can impact its long-term success and reputation.

Environmental

The Environmental aspect of ESG evaluates how a company manages its impact on the planet. This includes practices related to energy use, waste management, pollution control, natural resource conservation, and efforts to reduce carbon emissions. A high environmental score indicates that a company is effectively minimizing its ecological footprint and contributing positively to environmental sustainability.


Social

The Social component of ESG focuses on a company's relationships with its employees, customers, suppliers, and the communities where it operates. This includes labor practices, diversity and inclusion, human rights, community engagement, and customer satisfaction. A high social score suggests that a company prioritizes the well-being and fair treatment of its stakeholders, fostering a positive social impact.

Governance

Governance in ESG assesses the quality and transparency of a company's leadership, organizational structure, and business ethics. This includes factors such as board diversity, executive compensation, shareholder rights, and compliance with laws and regulations. A strong governance score reflects a company's commitment to ethical practices, accountability, and robust oversight mechanisms, ensuring long-term organizational integrity and stakeholder trust.
