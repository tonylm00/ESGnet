Before proceeding, familiarize yourself with the concepts of financial health scores that are part of our evaluation:

Piotroski Score: Developed by Joseph Piotroski, this score assesses a company's financial strength based on specific criteria. It evaluates factors such as profitability, leverage, operating efficiency, and cash flow to determine the overall health of the business. A higher Piotroski score typically indicates better financial health and potential for future growth.

Altman Z-Score: Created by Edward Altman, the Z-Score is a formula that predicts the likelihood of a company going bankrupt within two years. It combines multiple financial ratios to produce a single score, categorizing companies into distinct groups ranging from safe (high Z-Score) to distressed (low Z-Score). This score is crucial for assessing financial risk and stability.

Understanding these scores will help you interpret the financial health and stability of the companies you select or are evaluating.
